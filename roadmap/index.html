Tax Architecture

Taxes are a funding rail for MON rewards — not a gimmick.

Scope

  • Buy tax: Yes (5%)
  • Sell tax: Yes (5%)
  • Transfer tax: No
Status: Implemented (rates are parameter decisions)

Flow

  1. Tax collected in MMM
  2. MMM accumulates in token contract (taxTokens)
  3. Swap MMM → MON using router (batch execution)
  4. Send MON to Reward Tracker
  5. Holders claim MON from the tracker
Status: Implemented & tested end-to-end

Why Batch Swaps

  • Clear yield accounting (each swap is auditable)
  • Less noisy than reflections
  • Better anti-gaming control surfaces (you choose cadence/thresholds)
  • More predictable operations
Status: Implemented

Rewards System

Real MON rewards, distributed by on-chain snapshot accounting — not reflections.

How Rewards Are Generated

  1. Buy & sell taxes are collected in MMM
  2. MMM accumulates inside the token contract (taxTokens)
  3. swapTaxForRewards() swaps MMM → MON (batch)
  4. MON is transferred to the Reward Tracker
Status: Implemented & tested

How Rewards Are Distributed

  1. Tracker maintains an eligible supply (excludes specific addresses)
  2. Each MON deposit updates a global reward index (rewardPerTokenStored)
  3. Each wallet earns proportional to eligible MMM balance at reward time
  4. Rewards become claimable after the wallet is checkpointed (transfer/claim)
Status: Implemented & tested

Snapshot-Based Accounting

  • No reflections on every transfer
  • Passive holders do not pay constant gas
  • Rewards accrue against a global index
  • Balances are checkpointed on transfers
Status: Implemented

Anti-Abuse by Design

  • Tracker is notified at transfer level (checkpointing)
  • Excluded wallets do not earn (Pair, token contract, etc.)
  • Excluded wallets do not accrue rewards while excluded. Re-including does not retroactively accrue rewards for the excluded period. Rewards accrued before exclusion remain claimable.(test procedure documented)
  • High-frequency traders bleed via taxes and usually hold less
Status: Mostly implemented; "re-include no back-earn" should be proven by a dedicated test
